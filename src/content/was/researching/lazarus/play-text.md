<p class="h2 accent-overt" lang="la">Incipit Lazarus</p>

<dl class="drama" lang="enm">
<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>Commes now brethere and go With me:</dd>
<dd>We Will pas furth untill Iude.</dd>
<dd>To betany will we Weynde,</dd>
<dd>To vysit lazare, that is our freynde.</dd>
<dd>Gladly I wold we with hym speke;</dd>
<dd>I tell you sothely he is seke.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Petrus</dt>
<dd>I red not that ye thider go:</dd>
<dd>The Iues haldon you for thare fo.</dd>
<dd>I red ye com not in that stede,</dd>
<dd>ffor if ye do, than be ye dede.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Iohannes</dt>
<dd>Master, tryst thou [not] on the Iue,</dd>
<dd>ffor many day sen thou thaym knewe;</dd>
<dd>and last tyme that we were there</dd>
<dd>We wenyd till haue bene ded therfor.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Thomas</dt>
<dd>When we were last in that contre,</dd>
<dd>This othere day, both thou and we,</dd>
<dd>We wenyd that thou ther shuld haue bene slayn.</dd>
<dd>Will thou now go thider again?</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>Herkyn, breder, and takys kepe:</dd>
<dd>lazare oure freynde is fallyn on slepe.</dd>
<dd>The way till hym now will we take,</dd>
<dd>To styr that knyght and gar hym wake.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Petrus</dt>
<dd>Sir, me thynke it were best</dd>
<dd>To let hym slepe and take his rest,</dd>
<dd>and kepe that no man com hym hend.</dd>
<dd>ffor if he slepe, then mon he mend.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>I say to you: Withoutten fayll,</dd>
<dd>No kepyng may till hym availl,</dd>
<dd>Ne slepe may stand hym in no stede.</dd>
<dd>I say to you sekerly, he is dede.</dd>
<dd>Therfor, I say to you now at last:</dd>
<dd>leyfe this speche. go we fast.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Thomas</dt>
<dd>Sir, What so euer ye bid vs do,</dd>
<dd>We assent vs well therto.</dd>
<dd>I hope to god ye shall not fynde</dd>
<dd>None of vs lefe behynde.</dd>
<dd>ffor any parell that may befall,</dd>
<dd>Wend we With oure master all.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Martha</dt>
<dd>Help me, lorde, and gif me red:</dd>
<dd>lazare, my broder, now is dede,</dd>
<dd>That was to the both lefe and dere.</dd>
<dd>he had not dyed, had thou bene here.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>Martha, martha, thou may be fayn:</dd>
<dd>Thi brothere shall rise and lif agayn.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Martha</dt>
<dd>lorde, I wot that he shall ryse</dd>
<dd>And com before the iustyce,</dd>
<dd>ffor at the dredfull day of dome,</dd>
<dd>There mon ye kepe hym at his come,</dd>
<dd>To loke What dome ye Will hym gif.</dd>
<dd>Then mon he rise; then mon he lyf.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>I warne you, both man and wyfe,</dd>
<dd>That I am rysyng, and I am life;</dd>
<dd>And whoso truly trowys in me —</dd>
<dd>That I was euer, and ay shall be —</dd>
<dd>Oone thyng I shall hym gif:</dd>
<dd>Though he be dede, yit shall he lif.</dd>
<dd>Say thou, Woman, trowys thou this?</dd>

<dt lang="la">Martha</dt>
<dd>yee, for sothe, my lord of blys</dd>
<dd>Ellys were I greatly to mysprase.</dd>
<dd>ffor all is sothefast that thou saye.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>Go tell thy sister, mawdlayn,</dd>
<dd>That I com; ye may be fayn.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Martha</dt>
<dd>Sister, lefe this sorowful bande.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Oure lorde commys here at hand,</dd>
<dd>And hys apostyls with him also.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Maria</dt>
<dd>A, for godys luf, let me go.</dd>
<dd>Blissid be he that sende me grace,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">That I may se thee in this place.</dd>
<dd>lorde, mekill sorow may men se</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Of my sister here, and me.</dd>
<dd>We are heuy as any lede</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">ffor oure broder that thus is dede.</dd>
<dd>had thou bene here and on hym sene,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">dede, for soth, he had not bene.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>hider to yon commen we ar,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">To make you comforth of youre care.</dd>
<dd>Bot loke no fayntyse ne no slawth;</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Bryng yon out of stedfast trawthe —</dd>
<dd>Then shall I hold you that I saide.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">lo, where haue ye his body laide?</dd>

<dt lang="la">Maria</dt>
<dd>lorde, if it be thi Will,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">I hope be this he sauers ill,</dd>
<dd>ffor it is now the iiii day gone</dd>
<dd class="indent-3">sen he Was laide vnder yonde stone.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Ihesus</dt>
<dd>I told the right now ther thou stode,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">that thi trawth shuld ay be goode.</dd>
<dd>And if thou may that fulfill,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">All bees done right at thi Will.</dd>
<dd class="stage-direction" lang="la">Et lacrimatus est ihesu dicens</dd>
<dd>ffader, I pray the, that thou rase</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">lazare, that was thi hyne,</dd>
<dd>And bryng hym oute of his mysese,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And oute of hell pyne.</dd>
<dd>When I the pray, thou says allwayse,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">my Will is sich as thyne.</dd>
<dd>Therfor Will we now eke his dayse.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">To me thou Will inclyne.</dd>
<dd>Com furth, lazare, and stande vs by:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">In erth shall thou no langere ly.</dd>
<dd>Take and lawse hym foote and hand,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And from his throte take the bande,</dd>
<dd>And the sudary take him fro,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And all that gere, and let hym go.</dd>

<dt lang="la">Lazarus</dt>
<dd>lorde, that all thyng maide of noght</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">louyng be to the,</dd>
<dd>That sich Wonder here was Wroght:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Gretter may none be.</dd>
<dd>When I was dede to hell, I soght,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And thou, thrugh thi pauste,</dd>
<dd>Rasid me vp and thens me broght.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Behold, and ye may se.</dd>
<dd>There is none so styf on stede,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Ne none so prowde in prese,</dd>
<dd>Ne none so dughty in his dede,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Ne none so dere on deese —</dd>
<dd>No kyng, no knyght, no Wight in wede,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">ffrom dede haue maide hym seese —</dd>
<dd>Ne flesh he was wonte to fede:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">It shall be Wormes mese:</dd>
<dd>youre dede is Wormes coke.</dd>
<dd>youre myrroure here ye loke,</dd>
<dd>And let me be youre boke:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">youre sampill take by me.</dd>
<dd>ffro dede you cleke in cloke;</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">sich shall ye all be.</dd>
<dd>Ilkon in sich aray,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">With dede thai shall dight</dd>
<dd>And closid in clay.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Wheder he be kyng or knyght</dd>
<dd>ffor all his garmentes gay</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">that semely were in sight,</dd>
<dd>his flesh shall frete away</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">With many a wofull wight.</dd>
<dd>Then wofully sich wightys</dd>
<dd>Shall gnawe thise gay knyghts:</dd>
<dd>Thare lunges and thare lyghtys</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Thare harte shall frete in sonder.</dd>
<dd>Thise masters most of myghtys</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Thus shall then be broght vnder —</dd>
<dd>Vnder the erthe ye shall</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Thus carefully then cowche</dd>
<dd>The royse of youre hall;</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">youre nakyd nose shall towche.</dd>
<dd>Nawther great ne small</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">To you will kneele ne crowche.</dd>
<dd>A shete shall be youre pall.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Sich todys shall be youre nowche.</dd>
<dd>Todys shall you dere.</dd>
<dd>ffeyndys will you fere.</dd>
<dd>youre flesh that fare was here</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">thus rufully shall rote.</dd>
<dd>In stede of fare colore,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">sich bandys shall bynde youre throte,</dd>
<dd>youre rud that was so red,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">youre lyre, the lylly lyke,</dd>
<dd>Then shall be wan as led —</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And stynke as dog in dyke.</dd>
<dd>Wormes shall in you brede</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">As bees dos in the byke</dd>
<dd>And ees outt of youre hede.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Thusgate shall paddokys pyke —</dd>
<dd>To pike you are preste</dd>
<dd>Many vncomly beest;</dd>
<dd>Thus thai shall make a feste</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Of your flesh and of youre blode.</dd>
<dd>ffor you then sorowes leste</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">The moste has of youre goode;</dd>
<dd>youre goode ye shall forsake,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">If ye be neuer so lothe,</dd>
<dd>And nothyng With you take</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">But sich such a wyndyng clothe.</dd>
<dd>Youre Wife sorow shall slake,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">youre childer also both,</dd>
<dd>Vnnes youre mynnyng make,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">If ye be neuer so wrothe:</dd>
<dd>Thai myn you With nothyng</dd>
<dd>That may be youre helpyng,</dd>
<dd>Nawthere in mes syngyng,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Ne yit with almis dede.</dd>
<dd>Therfor in youre leuyng,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Be wise and take good hede,</dd>
<dd>Take hede for you to dele</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Whils ye ar on life.</dd>
<dd>Trust neuer fryndys freyle,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Nawthere of childe then Wife,</dd>
<dd>ffor sectures are not lele,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Then for youre good Will stryfe;</dd>
<dd>To by youre saules hele,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">There may no man thaym shrife.</dd>
<dd>To shrife man thaym may</dd>
<dd>After youre ending day:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">youre saull forto glad</dd>
<dd>youre sectures Will swere nay,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And say ye aght more then ye had.</dd>
<dd>Amende the man Whils thou may:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">let neuer no myrthe fordo thi mynde.</dd>
<dd>Thynke thou on the dredefullday</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">When god shall deme all mankynde.</dd>
<dd>Thynk thou farys as dothe wynde:</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">This warlde is wast & will away.</dd>
<dd>Man, haue this in thi mynde,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And amende the Whils that thou may.</dd>
<dd>Amende the man whils thou art here,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Agane thou go anothere gate,</dd>
<dd>When thou art dede and laide on bere,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Wit thou well, thou bees to late.</dd>
<dd>ffor if all the good that euer thou gate</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Were delt for the after thi day,</dd>
<dd>In heuen it wolde not mende thi state.</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">fforthi amende the Wils thou may.</dd>
<dd>If thou be right and ryall in rente</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">As in the stede standing in stall,</dd>
<dd>In thi harte knowe and thynk</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">That thai are goddys goodys</dd>
<dd>he myght haue maide the poore and small,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">As he that beggys fro day to day —</dd>
<dd>With thou well accountys, gif thou shall,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Therfor amende the whils thou may.</dd>
<dd>And if I myght with you dwell</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">To tell you all my tyme,</dd>
<dd>fful mekill couthe I tell,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">That I have harde and sene,</dd>
<dd>Of many a great meruell,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Sich as ye wolde not wene,</dd>
<dd>In the paynes of hell,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">Theras I haue bene.</dd>
<dd>Bene I haue in wo;</dd>
<dd>Therfor kepe you ther fro</dd>
<dd>Whilst ye lif — do so</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">If ye will dwell with hym,</dd>
<dd>That can gar you thus go</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And hele you lith and lym.</dd>
<dd>he is a lord of grace;</dd>
<dd>Vmthynke you in this case,</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And pray hym full of myght,</dd>
<dd>he kepe you in this place</dd>
<dd class="indent-1">And haue you in his sight.</dd>
<dd class="indent-2" lang="la">Amen.</dd>
</dl>
